What is a QR Code?

A QR (Quick Response) code is a two-dimensional barcode that can be scanned by any smartphone camera. When scanned, it instantly opens a website, starts a WhatsApp chat, initiates a UPI payment, connects to WiFi, or performs any other programmed action.

What is UPI QR Code?
A UPI QR code allows customers to pay you instantly using any UPI app — Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, BHIM, or any bank UPI app. When scanned, the payment app opens with your UPI ID and amount pre-filled. QRMaker is the only free generator for UPI QR codes.

Yes. All QR code generation happens entirely in your browser — no data is sent to any server. Your UPI ID, phone number, WiFi password — nothing is uploaded or stored anywhere.
What size should I print my QR code?
Minimum 2.5 cm × 2.5 cm for scanning at close range. For outdoor display (shop front, banner), use at least 10 cm × 10 cm. Download 2048px PNG or SVG for best print quality.
Can I customize the QR code color?
Yes! Use the design panel to change the foreground and background colors, choose dot styles (square, rounded, dots), and eye styles. Keep foreground dark and background light for best scanning reliability.
What is the difference between PNG and SVG?
PNG is a pixel image — great for digital use and general printing. SVG is a vector file — scales to any size without quality loss, perfect for large format printing. Both are free on QRMaker.
How do I scan a QR code?
Open your phone camera app and point it at the QR code. On iPhone, tap the notification. On Android, most camera apps detect QR codes automatically. Or use QRMaker's built-in QR Scanner to scan from camera or image upload.
